Introduction

Learn more →

The Solar System is our cosmic neighborhood centered on the Sun. It formed ~4.6 billion years ago and contains eight planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, meteoroids, comets, and distant icy bodies—each playing a role in a dynamic, beautiful system.

  • Bound by the Sun’s gravity
  • Eight planets + many small bodies
  • Extends beyond Neptune to the Kuiper Belt & Oort Cloud

History of the Solar System

Learn more →

A collapsing molecular cloud formed a spinning solar nebula with the Sun at its center. Collisions built planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. Later migrations and bombardments shaped today’s structure.

The Sun – Heart of the Solar System

Learn more →
  • G-type star, ~4.6 billion years old
  • ~99.86% of Solar System mass
  • Energy via nuclear fusion

The Planets

Learn more →

Eight planets: four rocky inner worlds (Mercury–Mars) and four outer giants (Jupiter–Neptune). Each has unique size, composition, atmosphere, and features.

Dwarf Planets

Learn more →

Nearly round bodies that orbit the Sun but haven’t cleared their orbits: Pluto, Eris, Haumea, Makemake, and Ceres (in the asteroid belt).

Moons

Learn more →

Over 290 confirmed moons. Some are icy with possible oceans (Europa, Enceladus), others are geologically diverse (Io, Titan). Earth’s Moon stabilizes our planet’s rotation and drives tides.

Asteroids, Meteoroids & Comets

Learn more →

Asteroids are rocky (mostly in the asteroid belt). Meteoroids are small fragments; meteors streak when they burn in our atmosphere. Comets are icy bodies that grow glowing tails near the Sun.

Regions of the Solar System

Learn more →

Inner rocky planets → Asteroid belt → Outer gas/ice giants → Kuiper Belt (icy objects, dwarf planets) → far Oort Cloud (long-period comets).
